Initial Analysis

Initially, in Census_and_Corp_Ownership_and_Occupancy_Over_Time, I thought there would be more variation in the race and age demographics throughout the years. However, at a cursory glance, it seems that all of the age and race demographics stay consistent (not changing at all). This is very surprising as I would expect that the number of people would change as the amount of houses/buildings change or as people move in and out. I am a bit confused on how people are being counted. There are multiple spreadsheets with this same data. In zip_code_neighborhood, leading zeros are not presented. No missing data. Race and age demographics are given as a count. Occupancy and ownership rates are given a float. I decided to remove all data that looks at facility type and race and age together. Not all neighborhoods present in either table are called the same thing.

In A2_EDA_CSV, it seems like non-investors sell to investors more than investors sell to investors. Flip horizon, flip_diff, month_horizon, price_diff_pch, style, prsldate, and restype_R1F have missing values. I removed these values as I do not think they are relevant to the questions I am asking. There are a lot of different columns (not all relevant), but data is presented as dates and integers. Some relevant values are investor_type_purchase, inv_to_inv, and investor_type_sale.

Discoveries & Insights

Summary

Overall, it seems that corpate ownership rates are skyrocketing. Certain neighborhoods have different rates but all of them are climbing. Generally, corporate ownership rates are associated with lower owner occupancy. Racial demographics are not very affected by corporate ownership rates very much. No matter what race you are higher corporate ownership means there are less people (and thus people like you) over there.
